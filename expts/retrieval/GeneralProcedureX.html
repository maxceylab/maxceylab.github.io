Dear participants,

Thank you for taking the time to look into this experiment. Before we begin, we want to inform you of a few things. The Institutional Review Board (IRB) is the board who oversees all experimentation ensuring that subjects rights are protected. Because we are not publishing the results of this experiment, or using presenting them outside of a classroom environment, the experiment in which you are about to participate is not beholden to IRB approval. While we are interested in the results, this experiment is meant to teach us about the research process as a whole. Not being under IRB approval does not mean you have no rights. All information collected is confidential and anonymous. Also, this is a noninvasive experiment where you will be asked simple questions regarding a series of images. Participation is completely voluntary (we are not gluing you to your computer and forcing you to complete it). At any time, you can stop the experiment and leave without any penalty.

We hope you continue the experiment. Thank you for your time!



I understand and agree to participate in this study

This experiment is a memorization task involving visual images. It will take approximately 30 - 45 minutes to complete.

Next (1 of 5)

You will be presented with 72 objects, one at a time, each presented for 5 seconds.

For example:

Study the color and identity of each object for a later memory test.
Every object will have a color associated with it that you will later be tested on.

Next (2 of 5)

Studied objects will often be similar to each other. For instance, you may encounter several different types of cookie jars.

It is therefore not sufficient to remember "purple cookie jar". Your memory will need to be as precise as "purple Garfield cookie jar".

After you have studied all the objects, we will test your memory.

Next (3 of 5)

There are two possible ways we may test your memory.

1. Recognition Test:

In Recognition test trials, a colored object is presented with two buttons beneath, "Old" and "New". For example:

If you have seen the object before (i.e., it was one of the 72 objects that you previously studied), then click the "Old" button. In this case, you previously saw this cookie jar so you would click "Old".

If you have never seen the object before, then click the "New" button.

Since you have not seen the above cookie jar before, you'd click the "New" button.

We will never trick you by presenting a previously seen object in the incorrect color. E.g., we would never present a yellow Garfield cookie jar.

Next (4 of 5)

2. Color Test:

In Color test trials, an object is presented in grayscale with two buttons beneath, "Old" and "New". For example:

Just like the Recognition test trials, if you have seen the object before, click the "Old" button. Otherwise, click the "New" button.

In this case, since you have seen the above object before, you would click the "Old" button (even though it is not presented in its original color).

After clicking one of the buttons, a color wheel will be presented. Move your mouse around the color wheel to change the color of the displayed object. Find the original color of the object and then click using your mouse to confirm your selection.

If you are unsure of the original color, move your mouse around and click what feels right. Please be as precise as possible in selecting what you think is the object's original color.

Even if you select "New", you will still need to select a color. This means that you will sometimes be asked to select the color of an object you have never seen before! When this happens, you can make a random selection on the color wheel to continue. We do this because it is possible you think the object is "New" when it was actually "Old", in which case the color you provide could still be correct.

Ready to start? You will begin by studying a series of 72 colored objects.

Please set aside approximately 45 minutes to complete this experiment. Please do your best -- the amount of time we will spend analyzing your data far exceeds 45 minutes!
